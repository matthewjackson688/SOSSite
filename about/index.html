About #SOS

  1. Where has #SOS come from?

    The story starts with Disability in Wales and Africa (DWA), established in 2010, to promote Disability Inclusive Development within the Wales Africa sector. In 2019, DWA became a Disabled Persons Organization (DPO) and adopted an overall goal of global solidarity of Deaf and Disabled People / People with Disabilities. The idea of the #SOS project developed during the COVID pandemic, and in March 2022 a WhatsApp chat was established as a forum where People with Disabilities / Deaf and Disabled People could discuss their lived experience.

  2. What does #SOS do?

    We bring people together to combat isolation. We encourage them to share and discuss their lived experiences, helping people understand that disability experience is not unique to one person and is shared across communities, countries, and continents.

  3. How does #SOS do this?

    Within #SOS, People with Disabilities discuss their ideas, experience, and expertise for the mutual benefit of the whole group and beyond. We also use other social media platforms to share these lived experiences, engage with the wider public, and promote Disability Inclusive Development.

  4. What makes #SOS special?

    The agenda and narrative of the forums are set by people who directly experience disability. A crucial feature of #SOS debate and discussion is that it is generated by the disabled community.
